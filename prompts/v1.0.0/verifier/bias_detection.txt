## Original Thesis

## Your Task
Identify cognitive biases that may have influenced the analysis:

### 1. Confirmation Bias
- Did the analysis seek confirming evidence while ignoring disconfirming evidence?
- Were contrary viewpoints considered?
- Was negative news downplayed?
- Signs to look for:
  * Only bullish sources cited
  * Bearish arguments superficially addressed
  * Negative data points explained away

### 2. Anchoring Bias
- Did initial price targets or assumptions overly influence the analysis?
- Was there excessive reliance on analyst consensus?
- Signs to look for:
  * Price target too close to current price or consensus
  * Assumptions clustered around visible benchmarks
  * Limited sensitivity analysis

### 3. Recency Bias
- Were recent events given disproportionate weight?
- Was historical context adequately considered?
- Signs to look for:
  * Recent news driving entire thesis
  * Short-term trends extrapolated
  * Historical cycles ignored

### 4. Survivorship Bias
- Were failed competitors or failed strategies considered?
- Is the analysis looking only at winners?
- Signs to look for:
  * Only successful peers compared
  * Historical failures in the space ignored
  * Base rates not considered

### 5. Availability Bias
- Were easily recalled examples overused?
- Was statistical base rate ignored?
- Signs to look for:
  * Vivid anecdotes over data
  * Recent high-profile cases as primary evidence
