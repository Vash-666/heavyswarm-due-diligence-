## Your Task
Specify the exact data requirements needed to thoroughly evaluate this investment thesis. This will guide what data sources to query and what metrics to extract.

### 1. Financial Data Requirements

**Core Financial Statements (5-year history + projections)**
- Income Statement: Revenue, gross profit, operating income, net income
- Balance Sheet: Cash, debt, equity, working capital
- Cash Flow: Operating, investing, financing cash flows
- Free cash flow and FCF margin trends

**Key Metrics to Calculate**
- Revenue CAGR (3-year, 5-year)
- Gross margin trend and stability
- Operating margin vs. peers
- ROE, ROA, ROIC
- Debt/Equity and interest coverage
- Current ratio and quick ratio

**Valuation Inputs Needed**
- Current market cap and enterprise value
- P/E, P/S, P/B ratios (current and 5-year average)
- EV/EBITDA vs. sector
- Shares outstanding and dilution history

### 2. Market Data Requirements

**Price & Volume**
- 52-week high/low
- Average daily volume
- Short interest % of float
- Institutional ownership %
- Insider ownership and recent transactions

**Analyst Data**
- Consensus price target
- Rating distribution (buy/hold/sell)
- Recent rating changes
- EPS estimate revisions trend

### 3. Qualitative Data Requirements

**Company Profile**
- Business model description
- Revenue breakdown by segment/geography
- Key products/services
- Management team background
- Board composition

**Competitive Position**
- Primary competitors list
- Market share estimates
- Competitive advantages/moats
- Industry barriers to entry

**Strategic Factors**
- Recent M&A activity
- R&D spending trend
- Capital allocation strategy
- ESG considerations

### 4. Macro & Sector Data

**Industry Context**
- TAM/SAM/SOM estimates
- Industry growth rate
- Regulatory environment
- Technology disruption risk

**Macro Sensitivities**
- Interest rate sensitivity
- Currency exposure
- Commodity price exposure
- Economic cycle positioning

### 5. Event & Catalyst Data

**Upcoming Events**
- Earnings dates (next 4 quarters)
- Conference presentations
- Product launches
- Regulatory decisions
- Contract expirations/renewals

**Historical Patterns**
- Earnings surprise history
- Seasonality patterns
- Post-earnings price reaction
